Text C
Leaflet

Are you looking for accommodation?

Some people think finding accommodation is extremely stressful, but keep calm and you'll get there. The best place to look is online: check out websites and decide whether you want a room in a house or your own flat. You can look in estate agents' windows and ask family and friends.

Don't fork out any cash until you've seen the property. Take your best friend when you visit, as it's safer and they may notice something you don't. If relevant, meet your future housemates to ensure you get on. Ask questions like:

  • how much is the rent?
  • does the rent include bills?
  • do you have to pay anything upfront?

See whether your friend has any additional questions.

Check the room is big enough for all your possessions and ensure things like the shower work properly. Calculate how long it will take you to travel to work or college; you could also investigate whether the neighbours are noisy.

Think carefully before reaching a decision and ask your best mate for their opinion. Once you've decided, read the contract thoroughly so you know what you're committing yourself to before you sign it and keep a copy in a safe place. Then you can arrange to collect the keys. If it's not suitable, hang on in there – you'll discover the perfect place eventually.
