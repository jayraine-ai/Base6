Word of the Week · Base 6

Trajectory

/ trəˈdʒek.tər.i /

noun
trajectory (noun)
The curved path that an object follows as it moves through the air or space — shaped by the force that launched it and the pull of gravity.

Mission Control · Houston

Every rocket, satellite and space probe ever launched has followed a trajectory.

Engineers at NASA spend years calculating the exact trajectory a spacecraft needs to follow — the right angle, the right speed, the right curve through space. Get it wrong by even a fraction and a probe misses its target by millions of miles.

Anatomy of a trajectory

Trajectory in the real world

🚀 Space
Rocket to the Moon
Scientists plotted the trajectory of Apollo 11 so it would curve perfectly into lunar orbit.
⚽ Sport
Free Kick
The goalkeeper watched the trajectory of the ball curl up and over the wall.
🌧️ Weather
Raindrops
Wind changes the trajectory of falling rain, making it hit at an angle instead of straight down.
🎯 Life
Life Path
People sometimes say your choices today shape the trajectory of your future.

The word in a sentence

Everyday
She watched the trajectory of the stone as it arced through the air and splashed into the lake.
Science
The trajectory of the probe was adjusted mid-flight to avoid an asteroid field.
Metaphor
The team's trajectory this season has been impressive — they've won six games in a row.
