What is Data?

📋 Data is just information

Data is any collection of facts, numbers or observations. We gather data to help us understand the world — to spot patterns, answer questions and make decisions.

Data = collected information that can be counted, measured or described.

For example: the number of students who prefer different types of music, the temperatures recorded each day for a week, or how many people visit a website each hour.

📊 Why do we use charts?

Raw data (just a list of numbers) can be hard to understand quickly. Charts and graphs turn data into pictures that are much easier to read at a glance.

Different charts are good for different things. Choosing the right chart for your data is an important skill.

📊 Bar Charts

How does a bar chart work?

A bar chart uses rectangular bars to represent data. The taller the bar, the bigger the value. Bar charts are great for comparing different categories.

Every bar chart needs: a title, labels on both axes, and a consistent scale on the vertical axis.

🥧 Pie Charts

What is a pie chart?

A pie chart shows data as slices of a circle. The whole circle represents 100% of the data. The bigger the slice, the bigger that category's share of the total.

Pie charts are best when you want to show how something is divided up — like what proportion of students chose each option in a survey.

Use a bar chart to compare amounts. Use a pie chart to show proportions of a whole.

🔍 Reading a pie chart

A class of 30 students was asked: "How do you travel to school?" Here are the results:

Walk — 12 students (40%)
Car — 9 students (30%)
Bus — 6 students (20%)
Cycle — 3 students (10%)

1. Which is the most common way to travel to school?

2. How many students travel by bus or cycle combined?

3. What percentage of students travel by car?
