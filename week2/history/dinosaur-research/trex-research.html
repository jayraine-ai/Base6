Step 1 · Warm-up fact check

Are these T. rex claims true?

Read each claim. Use what you know — or look it up — then pick your answer. Research the correct answer if you get one wrong.

T. rex had the strongest bite force of any land animal that has ever lived.

Think carefully — scientists have measured this and compared it to crocodiles and other dinosaurs.

T. rex was a fast runner that could reach speeds of over 40 mph.

This is a common claim — but recent fossil and biomechanics research has changed what scientists think.

T. rex may have had feathers, at least when it was young.

Look for research about T. rex skin impressions and what scientists have found about its close relatives.

T. rex lived during the Jurassic Period.

Check a timeline of geological periods — there are three main ones where dinosaurs lived.

T. rex arms were completely useless.

Scientists have debated this. Think about what those small arms might still have been able to do.

T. rex fossils have only ever been found in the USA.

Think about which countries have produced T. rex fossil discoveries — North America is large.

⚠️ Common mistake: Many people spell it Tyranosaurus or Tyrannasaurus. The correct spelling is Tyrannosaurus — two n's and two s's.
