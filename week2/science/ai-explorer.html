What is Artificial Intelligence?

🧠 Intelligence — but artificial

When we say something is intelligent, we usually mean it can think, learn and solve problems. Humans do this naturally. But what if a machine could do it too?

Artificial Intelligence — or AI — is when computers are programmed to do things that normally require human thinking. Things like recognising your face, understanding what you say, or suggesting what to watch next.

AI = computers that can learn, recognise patterns, and make decisions.

🤔 Is AI the same as a robot?

Not always! Robots are physical machines — they have bodies. AI is the brain, not the body. Some robots use AI, but AI can also live inside your phone, your TV, or a website — with no body at all.

Think of it this way: a calculator does maths but it's not AI — it just follows fixed rules. An AI can learn from experience and get better over time. That's the key difference.

📚 A quick definition to remember

AI is technology that can learn from information and use what it has learned to do useful things — like recognising speech, spotting patterns, or making suggestions.

AI in Everyday Life

AI is everywhere — you've probably used it today without even realising. Here are some real examples:

🎵
Spotify / YouTube
AI learns what music and videos you enjoy and suggests more like them
🗣️
Voice Assistants
Siri, Alexa and Google Assistant use AI to understand what you're saying
📸
Face Unlock
Your phone uses AI to recognise your face and unlock
🛒
Online Shopping
AI suggests products based on what you've looked at before
🎮
Video Games
Enemy characters and NPCs use AI to react to what you do
🌐
Google Search
AI helps rank and suggest the most useful results for your search
🚗
Sat Nav
AI predicts traffic and finds the best route in real time
💬
Chatbots
Customer service bots and AI assistants like me use AI to have conversations
