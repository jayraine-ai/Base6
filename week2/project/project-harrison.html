🧵 Ways to Attach the Parts

You can combine more than one method. Click each section to read the options.

  • Wrap method: wrap fabric, string, cork or foam around the handle area, then tie it off.
  • Tape method: use reused tape, masking tape or electrical tape to hold the grip in place.
  • Slot method: if using cork, cut a small slot and press pieces around the rod, then wrap them.
  • Glue method: use glue only if allowed, and let it dry fully before testing.
  • Thread wrap: hold the loop against the rod and wrap thread tightly around the feet of the loop.
  • Tape wrap: tape the bottom of each loop down, leaving the hole clear for the line.
  • Wire twist: twist thin wire around the rod carefully, then cover sharp ends with tape.
  • Glue plus wrap: use a tiny amount of glue, then wrap thread or tape around it for extra strength.
  • Fixed line holder: tie or tape a spool to the rod so the line wraps around it.
  • Simple spinning reel: push a pencil, dowel or skewer through a spool so it can turn.
  • Cardboard bracket: make a U-shape support from cardboard, then tape it to the rod.
  • Bottle lid reel: sandwich cardboard between two lids, make a centre hole, use a pencil as the axle.
  • Tie method: tie the line to the rod tip with a simple knot.
  • Guide method: thread the line through each eyelet from the reel to the tip.
  • Practice fishing method: tie a magnet or paperclip shape to the line for a safe indoor test.

🧩 Problems I Might Solve

If the rod is too bendy...

Try a thicker pole, tape two thin poles together, or shorten the rod.

If the guides fall off...

Use more wraps, add tape over the wraps, or use glue plus thread.

If the reel won't spin...

Make the hole bigger, use a smoother axle, or make it a fixed holder instead.

If the handle slips...

Add more wrapping, use rougher fabric, or put tape underneath the grip.

💡 Safe Alternatives

🧲 Instead of a sharp hook

Use a magnet on the end. Make cardboard fish with paper clips attached — works just as well for testing!

✂️ Instead of cutting wire

Bend paper clips gently by hand into loops. No cutting needed and they work brilliantly as eyelets.

🔨 Instead of nails or screws

Wrap with thread or string, then tape over it. Just as strong and much safer to do without tools.
