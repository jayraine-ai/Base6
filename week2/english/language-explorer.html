Language Features: Adventure Worksheets

Jay's Language Explorer Activity Companion

Name: ______________________ Date: ___________

Activity 1: Matching Tools (Memory Cave)

Draw lines to match each language feature with its correct definition.

Simile Directly saying one thing is another thing to paint a strong picture.
Metaphor Giving human actions or feelings to something that isn't human.
Personification Words that sound exactly like the physical noise they make.
Onomatopoeia Comparing two things directly using the words 'like' or 'as'.

Activity 2: The Detective (Understand & Spot)

Read the sentences carefully. Circle or underline the target language feature.

"The ancient trees whispered dark secrets to the passing clouds."

Target feature to find: Personification. Circle the words that prove this.

"Slippery silver snakes slithered smoothly through the swamp."

Target feature to find: Alliteration. Circle the repeated sounds.

Activity 3: Explorer's Analysis (Scale Impact)

A writer says: "The lightning was a jagged sword slicing through the velvet night."

1. What is the writer comparing the lightning to? ___________________________________________

2. Why did the writer use "velvet" to describe the night? How does that change the feeling?
