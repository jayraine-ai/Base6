🏺 The Evidence Room

Three artefacts from beyond living memory have arrived at the museum, and you're the historian on duty. The trick of the trade: knowing the difference between what the evidence shows, what's a sensible inference — and what's just a guess.

The historian's golden rule

Artefacts don't come with labels. Historians build the story in three layers:

🔍 The evidence shows it — you can see or read it directly on the object.
🧠 Sensible inference — not written on the object, but a fair conclusion from it.
❓ Just a guess — the evidence can't tell us this, however fun it sounds.
