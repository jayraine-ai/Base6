⚔️ Whose Story?

Nearly 2,000 years ago, Queen Boudicca led Britons in revolt against Rome. Here are two accounts of the same events — and they don't agree at all. Your job: work out whose side each writer is on, and why that matters.

First, the facts both sides agree on

Around AD 60, after Roman officials seized Iceni lands and mistreated Queen Boudicca and her daughters, she led a huge uprising. Her forces destroyed three Roman towns, including Londinium (London), before the Roman army defeated them in a final battle. Everything beyond that... depends on who's telling the story.

Source A

Written from the Roman side
Based on accounts by Roman writers
"The savage rebels swept across the land, burning the fine cities Rome had built. Led by the fearsome Boudicca, the lawless mob showed no mercy to civilised people. At last our brave legions, though greatly outnumbered, crushed the uprising and restored peace and order to the province."
