Word of the Week

⚙️ Optimise

Say it: OP · tim · eyes  ·  verb

What does it mean?

Optimise — to make something as good, effective or efficient as it can possibly be.

When you optimise something, you are not just improving it a little. You are tuning it, adjusting it and testing it until it works at its very best.

Where does it come from? The Latin word optimus, which means "best". So to optimise literally means "to make the best".

The word family

optimise (verb) — to make as good as possible optimal (adjective) — the best possible optimum (noun) — the best amount or level optimisation (noun) — the process of making something its best

Fun connection: an optimist is someone who expects the best to happen. Same Latin root, same idea — the best.

The word in the wild — this week

🧲 Science: Engineers optimise the shape of a rocket so the forces of drag slow it down as little as possible.
🦋 Physical World: We can optimise our conservation area by choosing the features that attract the most wildlife.
💻 Digital Media: AI systems are optimised by training them on huge amounts of data until their answers improve.
🔢 Maths: Knowing place value helps you optimise the way you calculate — finding the quickest, most accurate method.
🧘 Wellbeing: A few minutes of yoga can optimise how ready your body and mind feel for the day.
