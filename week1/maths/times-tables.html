Warm Up: What is a Times Table?

🧠 Small steps first

A times table is a pattern of equal groups. Instead of adding the same number again and again, multiplication helps us count faster.

6 × 4 means 4 groups of 6. It is the same as 6 + 6 + 6 + 6.

This lesson avoids pressure. You can use repeated addition, fingers, dots, drawing, skip counting or known facts. The goal is confidence, not speed.

Learning support: short sections, clear buttons, readable font, repeated structure, optional calm mode, movement breaks and no sudden sounds.

Build an Array

🔲 Arrays show equal groups

An array is a set of dots arranged in rows and columns. It helps us see multiplication as a picture.

6 rows of 4 dots = 24 dots altogether.

Fact Families

🔁 Turn facts around

Multiplication facts can be reversed. This is called the commutative rule.

6 × 4 = 24, so 4 × 6 = 24 too.

Knowing one fact gives you another fact for free.
